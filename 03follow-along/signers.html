Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Main author of the Declaration. Later became the 3rd President of the United States. A leading voice for American republicanism and Enlightenment ideals.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Strong advocate for independence. Later became the 2nd President of the United States. Known for his role in diplomacy and helping to draft the Declaration.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Senior statesman and prominent Enlightenment thinker. Renowned scientist, inventor, and diplomat. Helped negotiate the Treaty of Paris, which ended the Revolutionary War.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress. Famous for his large and bold signatureâ€”the most prominent on the document. His name became synonymous with "signature."
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Cousin of John Adams. A major leader in the movement for American independence. Organizer of the Boston Tea Party and member of the Sons of Liberty.
Richard Henry Lee (Virginia)
Introduced the resolution calling for independence from Britain. Prominent member of the Lee family, influential in early American politics.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, and act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
